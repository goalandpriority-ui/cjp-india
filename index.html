Tamil Nadu Fishermen — Sri Lanka Crisis
530 arrested in 2024 • 71 boats seized • Highest in 7 years

Tamil Nadu fishermen have been fishing in the Palk Bay for centuries. After the 1974 Indo-Lanka Maritime Agreement, India surrendered Katchatheevu Island. Now Sri Lankan Navy arrests TN fishermen — 530 arrested in 2024 alone, 71 boats seized. Some sentenced to 6 months to 1 year jail. Families destroyed. Livelihoods gone. PM Modi promised a solution in 2014. 11 years passed.

Root Cause: Katchatheevu given away by Emergency-era agreement. No diplomatic teeth from centre. IMBL disputes unresolved. TN fishermen use bottom trawling that damages Sri Lankan reef — adding to tension.
🪳 CJP FixNegotiate Katchatheevu 100-year lease from Sri Lanka — Tin Bigha model used with Bangladesh. Joint India-Sri Lanka fishing zone in Palk Bay. Indian Coast Guard rapid response: any arrested fisherman released within 72 hours or diplomatic escalation. Bottom trawling phased out — fishermen given modern deep-sea vessels. ₹5,000 crore fishermen welfare fund. Unemployment compensation for every arrest day.

Agriculture System Collapse
50%+ farm households in debt • Input costs rose 200% • No MSP law

The government announces MSP but buying below MSP has no legal penalty. Input costs rose 200% in 10 years. Farmer income barely moved. The farm loan waiver is a band-aid; structural reform is what's needed.

Root Cause: Fake seed/fertilizer companies operate openly. No direct market access. APMC monopolies. Land fragmentation — average farm size 1.08 hectares, too small for profit.
🪳 CJP FixBuying below MSP: buyer arrested. Fake agri-input companies: factory sealed, owner gets 10 years. e-APMC: farmers sell directly online to any buyer — middlemen become optional. Land consolidation cooperatives with government facilitation. Free soil testing + crop advisory in every panchayat. Natural farming transition support: ₹15,000/hectare subsidy (ref: Andhra Pradesh's zero-budget natural farming).
